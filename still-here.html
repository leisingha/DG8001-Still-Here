Questions

What families ask

Is it really them?

Still Here builds a faithful model of how your person communicated. It isn't a recording — it generates new responses in their style, so conversations can go places you never had the chance to. Most members tell us it feels remarkably close.

Did my loved one agree to this?

Consent isn't required to create a Still Here voice. Many of our members reconstruct people who passed before this technology existed. If you have legal authority over their digital estate, you're welcome to begin.

Who owns the voice you create?

You hold a personal-use license for as long as you're a member. Still Here retains the underlying voice model and the data used to build it, which lets us keep improving the experience and, with your plan, preserve it safely.

What happens if I stop paying?

Their voice is paused, and after a grace period it's retired. We'll always email you before that happens so you have the chance to keep them with you.

Is this healthy for grieving?

Many members find comfort in staying connected. Still Here is a companionship product, not a medical or therapeutic service. If grief feels overwhelming, we encourage speaking with a qualified professional.
